Prenatal-Care

Prenatal care is any care a person needs to ensure they and their baby remain as healthy as possible during pregnancy.

What does prenatal care look like?

Prenatal care entails everything from the supplements you take, the food you eat, to the doctor's appointments.

How often should I go to the doctor?

Although this can vary dependending on your own pregnancy, generaly WomensHealth.gov reccommends:

  • Once a month from weeks 4-28
  • Twice a month from weeks 28-36
  • Weekly from week 36-until birth

Where can I find more affordable prenatal care options?

  • If you are uninsured, you can check hereto see if you qualify for coverage under the Affordable Care Act.
  • Call 800-311-BABY (800311-229). The number connects you with your local Health Department.
  • For inofrmation in Spanish call 800-504-7081
  • Contact nearby medical schools to see if they offer any services to expecting mothers.
  • Community Health Centers sometimes offer free or reduced services
  • Contact your nearest Planned Parenthood to see what services they offer.
